Where we are today

The booking flow is clunky — and we're flying blind.

The current system relies on a third-party vendor API that exposes a tiny slice of the data we care about. Colleagues book a desk and hope; leadership reports on what the vendor decides to share.

Vendor lock-in

A black-box API drives our most-used workplace tool. We can't extend it, can't reshape it, can't get the metrics out.

No visibility on the day

Colleagues don't know who's in, which floor is busy, or whether their team is sitting together until they arrive.

Reporting gap

Line managers and leadership can't see utilisation, no-show patterns, or team behaviour without raising a vendor ticket.

Our concept

One product. Two surfaces.

Spaces@LBG is a workplace-owned desk-booking system, built around the live floorplan and the data we want to keep. A flat, accessible browser app for desk-based planning, plus a glassmorphism mobile app for the morning commute.

Prompt 3 — No-shows

One-hour cut-off. After that, the waitlist wins.

If someone books but doesn't show, who gets the desk and when?

A self-healing schedule.

  • Floor full? Join the waitlist for that floor in one tap.
  • One-hour check-in window — if you haven't checked in within an hour of your booking start, the desk is put back into circulation automatically.
  • If someone cancels, the waitlist fires immediately.
  • Alerts honour colleague preferences: push, email, or both.

Prompt 4 — Teams & Neighbourhoods

Sit with your team — without the WhatsApp coordination.

How do people who want to sit together actually end up together?

One button: Book next to my team.

  • See your teammates' bookings highlighted in blue on the floorplan.
  • Tap "Sit with team" — we pick the nearest open desk in that cluster.
  • Book for multiple colleagues in one flow (e.g. a PA booking for the leadership team).
  • Auto clash warning on double-bookings — before you commit, not after.

Prompt 5 — Admin & Policy

Three licenses. Driven by Workday. Owned by us.

How should leadership see utilisation without vendor-supplied reporting?

License tier is determined by the job-title marker on Workday — no manual provisioning, no separate access list. Reporting lives where the bookings live, so leadership stops waiting on vendor tickets.

Normal User

Default
  • Book multiple desks
  • Desks up to 4 months in advance
  • Meeting rooms up to 3 months in advance

Line Manager

Title marker = manager
  • Everything a Normal User can do
  • Stats tab: direct reports' booking frequency
  • Track no-shows & cancellations across the team

PA

Title marker = PA / EA
  • Everything a Normal User can do
  • Desks & meeting rooms up to 1 year in advance
  • Book on behalf of multiple execs in one flow

Prompt 6 — Accessibility

Nobody has to disclose anything. Everyone gets the controls.

What does a booking flow look like that works for everyone?

  • Amenities are universal. Anyone can filter for sit/stand, monitor, quiet zone, step-free — no "request for accommodation" gate.
  • Airbnb-style amenity cards — visual, scannable, easy to read.
  • Personal preferences in settings shape the suggestions you see first.
  • Light / dark mode toggle on every screen.
  • Adjustable font size in settings — respects system text scaling.
  • Two visual styles, one design system, both WCAG-AA in contrast and focus order.

Wrap-up

Workplace-owned. Data-rich. Accessible by default.

What we've shown today

A floorplan-first booking experience, flexible time windows with handover etiquette, a self-healing waitlist, team-aware booking, Workday-driven licensing with built-in reporting, and accessibility controls available to everyone — all on a flat browser app and a glassmorphism mobile app.

What's next

Replace the vendor API with our own service layer, integrate with Workday job markers for license assignment, and pilot on one floor of one building before scaling. Reporting becomes a first-class citizen, not a quarterly favour.
